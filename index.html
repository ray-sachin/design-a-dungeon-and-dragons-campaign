---END FILE--- >>> README.md # Aether Crown — A Solo 5e-Compatible Campaign A browser-based, mobile-friendly solo adventure with an integrated dice roller and lightweight tools. Your character can mend a fractured sky or seize a world-shaping artifact to rule. This project provides an original campaign storyline and play aids that are compatible with the standard Dungeons & Dragons 5e rules. It does not reproduce any official rule text. For rules and character options, use the official Basic Rules: - Basic Rules (2024): https://www.dndbeyond.com/sources/dnd/br-2024 ## Features - Solo-friendly branching campaign with meaningful choices: - Save civilians, aid the Wardens, stabilize shards to earn Renown. - Exploit chaos, hoard shards, break opponents to build Infamy. - A dynamic leaning determines the finale: Savior or Conqueror. - Built-in dice roller: - Supports NdM+K syntax (e.g., `1d20+5`, `2d6+1`) and advantage/disadvantage for d20. - Quick buttons for common dice and a persistent roll log. - Lightweight character panel: - Track level, proficiency bonus, ability modifiers, and skill proficiencies. - Supplies tracker and notes field. - Quick enemy templates for impromptu combat: - Add Minion, Skirmisher, Brute, Adept, or Elite foes with HP tracking. - Mobile responsive layout that works on phones and desktops. - Save/Load progress using localStorage. ## How to Play 1. Open `index.html` in a modern browser (desktop or mobile). 2. Bring a legal 5e character built with the Basic Rules. Enter your character’s name, level, proficiency, and ability modifiers in the Character panel. Check skill proficiencies you possess. 3. Read the scene text and click choices. When a choice shows a check (e.g., “DEX Stealth DC 13”), the app will roll a d20 and apply your relevant modifier automatically: - If the option lists a skill, your ability modifier and proficiency (if checked) will be added. - If it lists only an ability (e.g., CON), the relevant ability modifier is used. 4. Use the Dice Roller panel for any other rolls your class or the situation calls for. For advantage/disadvantage on d20 checks, select the mode before rolling. 5. Track supplies and make rests per the official rules. The app includes a note to remind you to reduce Supplies on a long rest. 6. For combats, use the Quick Enemies tool to add simple foes with suggested AC/HP/attack/damage, or substitute any appropriate SRD-compatible monsters. Roll attacks and damage using the Dice Roller. ## Campaign Overview - Title: The Aether Crown - Premise: A cosmic crown shattered, and its shards tore a rift over the city of Vesperreach. You can stabilise the world by returning and harmonising the fragments—or bind them to your will and enthrone yourself. - Structure: - Prologue: Choose how you respond to the initial disaster (heroic aid, opportunistic theft, pursuit, or shard study). - Act I: Travel to the Lodegate ruins and secure knowledge of three shard sites. - Act II: Visit sites in any order: - The Glass Mire: illusions and a mirror-hag. Options for investigation, endurance, bargaining, or combat. - The Ember Foundry: stealth or strength amid constructs; chance to craft a one-use boon. - The Sky Monastery: diplomacy or daring acrobatics with wind-sung monks. - Act III: The Halcyon Spire. Your Renown vs. Infamy leaning gates your finale: - Savior: Mend the Veil through a series of checks and potential guardian fights. - Conqueror: Intimidate, sabotage, or fight your way through the Wardens to bind the Crown and rule. Success or failure in checks steers the story and alters your Renown, Infamy, and Shard count. When you collect three or more shards, the final Convergence opens. ## Difficulty and DCs - DC suggestions follow standard 5e guidance: Easy 10, Medium 13, Hard 16, Very Hard 20. - In solo play, consider: - Using terrain and creativity to offset the swinginess of single-character combat. - Granting one free Inspiration at the start of each Act. - Treating some encounters as skill challenges rather than strict combats. ## Encounters The app includes simple enemy templates to support quick, theater-of-the-mind fights: - Minion: AC 12, HP 7, +3 to hit; 1d6+1 damage - Skirmisher: AC 13, HP 18, +4 to hit; 1d8+2 damage - Brute: AC 14, HP 45, +5 to hit; 1d10+3 damage - Adept (caster): AC 12, HP 27, Spell +5 / DC 13 - Elite: AC 15, HP 85, +6 to hit; 2d8+3 damage Adjust numbers to match your level, class, and magic items. Alternatively, pick any appropriate SRD-compatible monsters and use the Dice Roller for attacks, saves, and damage. ## Saving and Loading - Click Save to store your progress in your browser. - Click Load to resume. - Click New to start over. Data is saved locally using `localStorage` under the key `aetherCrownSave`. ## Accessibility and Mobile - Large, high-contrast buttons and inputs. - Responsive layout stacks panels on small screens. - Roll log is live-region friendly for screen readers. ## Safety and Content Themes include disaster response, mild body horror (mirror illusions), and moral choices around power vs. responsibility. As a solo player, you control pacing; use lines/veils as needed for your comfort. ## Legal - This is a fan-made adventure with original text. It references the 5e rules but does not reproduce rule text. - Dungeons & Dragons and related marks are trademarks of Wizards of the Coast. - Use the official Basic Rules for character creation and core mechanics: https://www.dndbeyond.com/sources/dnd/br-2024 ## Development Notes - All logic, styling, and data are embedded in `index.html` to keep deployment simple. - No external libraries are required. - You can expand the campaign by adding new scenes in the `scenes` object and referencing them via `goto` or `check` entries.
